Essential Question

What were the struggles and conflicts of the Middle Ages and the ways in which political order was achieved, including the role of the Catholic Church?

Struggles and Conflicts

There are many struggles and conflicts in Medieval European history, take as an example of the Black Death and the Crusades.

Struggles

The Black Death was a type of bacteria that in its worst case, cause fingers and other useless limbs to turn black, if this happened, it is nearly confirmed that you will die. It is most commonly known for killing 1/3 of Europe, it was eradicated due to the herd immunity law, social distancing, and other unknown factors. This was a huge struggle since it caused an early death to millions of people.

Conflicts

There were also conflicts, such as the 3 crusades. The crusades were mostly about ruling the “holy land” which was also known as Jerusalem. Jerusalem was holy to Muslims, Jews, and Christens for many reasons. For Christens, Jesus practiced miracles, was crucified, and rose from the dead here. There is also the Church of Holy Sepulchre. For Muslims, Mohammed rose from heaven in Jerusalem and the Dome of Rock is there. For Jews, the capital of the Jewish nation is here and was the location of the first temple, which was established by King Solomon. In the end, 4 things about medieval Europe change. The power of the pope increased, and subsequently did the church, breakdown of feudalism and growth of cities, beginning of loans and subsequently interest/credit, and new middle-eastern products were starting to sprinkle in the European market such as pepper, muslin, chess, algebra, coffee, rice, and more.

Political Order

In medieval times, there was feudalism. Feudalism is a structure of power and roles that consisted of a King/Queen, Lords and Vassals, Knights (and their structure), Commoners, and Serfs (Slaves that owe a debt). Kings\Queens were at the top of the food chain and ruled the land. Lords and Vassals are right under the king and one of their most important jobs is to serve the king and he did so by paying his taxes and supplying him with his finest soldiers. Knights started as Pages where they became Squires at age 14, and they started to live with lords at age 7. Knighthood is only given if he is deserving of it, and is treated as the inner circle of the best warriors. Commoners were everyday people in the village and Serfs were slaves that had a debt. When their debt was paid, they would be free.

Role of the Church

In the Middle Ages, churches held a lot of political power (History of the Church in the Middle Ages). In addition to being a religious institution sometimes, in the case of the Pope, hold more power than a king or queen. The church had laws and can take taxes, in addition to having its punishments (sometimes executions!). Churches were very much to be feared in the Middle Ages. In addition to this, they also play a role in the Black Death, Feudalism, and the Crusades.

Role of the Church in the Black Death

The church pleaded that the Black Death, or what they called the “Pestilence”, was God punishing people for their sins. They told people to pray and even organized religious marches to stop the plague.

Role of the Church in Feudalism

In feudalism, most of the population was catholic and bishops played a huge role. They usually hold very valuable land and also lead the people, religiously. The church also uses a type of feudalism inside as a power structure, the Pope is the King, the Bishop/Cardinal is second, and much more.

Role of the Church in the Crusades

The Crusades were a series of religious battles so if the Church wasn’t involved somehow, there would be a big problem. The church was in a battle with the Jews and Muslims over control of the “holy land” also known as Jerusalem. While it’s holy for different reasons to each group, for the Church, it’s where Jesus performed miracles and was crucified/brought back to life here. In the end, it impacted all who participated and changed how Muslims look at christens and “other hostile forces.”
